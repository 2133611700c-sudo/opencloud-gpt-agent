# Primary Source Verification Protocol

Date: 2026-06-15
Status: mandatory

## Rule

Use the newest available primary source for every material claim. Commercial directories, search snippets, social networks, and AI summaries are discovery tools only.

## Priority

1. Federal statutes, regulations, agencies, courts, and official datasets.
2. State licensing boards, Secretaries of State, statutes, and agency records.
3. County and city licensing, permits, DBA, procurement, and open-data systems.
4. Official company filings and current company websites.
5. Current vendor documentation and published pricing.
6. Industry research with disclosed methodology.

## Federal sources

- U.S. Census Bureau: County Business Patterns, Economic Census, Statistics of U.S. Businesses, Business Formation Statistics, Census API.
- Bureau of Labor Statistics: OEWS, QCEW, employment and wage data for NAICS 238220.
- SBA: current small-business definitions and reports.
- FCC and FTC: current call, text, telemarketing, consent, and do-not-call rules.
- Federal Register and eCFR: current rule text and effective dates.
- Official court opinions when current litigation changes the legal interpretation.

## Initial state sources

- California: Contractors State License Board and Secretary of State bizfile.
- Arizona: Registrar of Contractors and Corporation Commission.
- Texas: Department of Licensing and Regulation, State Board of Plumbing Examiners, and official entity records.
- North Carolina: Board of Examiners of Plumbing, Heating and Fire Sprinkler Contractors and Secretary of State.
- Relevant city and county permit, contractor-registration, DBA, and open-data systems where available.

## Company verification

Before commercial review, verify where accessible:

- legal or licensed name;
- active entity or license status;
- plumbing or HVAC classification;
- official website match;
- current public contact path;
- service geography;
- signs of current operation;
- existing answering, booking, CRM, field-service, or call-tracking solution.

A trade name absent from an entity search is not automatically false. Mark uncertainty rather than inventing a match.

## Freshness

- Laws, rules, prices, product features, licenses, and entity status: verify at retrieval time.
- Company websites and contacts: verify on the review date.
- Market statistics: use the newest official release and record its reporting period.
- Reports older than 18 months require an explicit age warning.
- Never combine periods without labeling each period.

## Evidence record

Store source authority, URL or record identifier, publication or reporting date, retrieval timestamp, exact supported fact, confidence, conflicts, and unresolved assumptions.

## Conflict rule

Current official license, entity, and rule records override directories, marketing pages, and old articles. When official sources conflict or are incomplete, mark the fact UNRESOLVED.

## Access limits

Some databases require accounts, fees, manual verification, public-record requests, or prohibit bulk extraction. Do not bypass controls. Record incomplete coverage honestly.

## Report metrics

Every validation report must disclose official-source coverage, verified license/entity counts, unresolved identities, reporting periods, inaccessible sources, manual-review count, money spent, messages sent, and current validation gate.
